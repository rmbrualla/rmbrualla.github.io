Ricardo Martin-Brualla

I work at Google, where I lead an effort of recovering 3D for all items on Google Search. We are hiring research scientists and software engineers, please reach out if you are interested in a role!

Previously at Google, I worked on Project Starline, a 3D telepresence system.

I did my PhD in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Washington in 2016 with Steve Seitz as my advisor. and my bachelors at Barcelona Tech / UPC. I taught Computer Vision at the University of Washington in 2018. I grew up in Madrid and attended the Deutsche Schule Madrid, where I learned a lot of German.

I love traveling and seeing other parts of the world, ideally by human power. In 2010, I traveled by bicycle around New Zealand, visiting the antipodes of my house in Madrid. I also went to Vanuatu and climbed a remote volcano to see lava bubbling. In 2016, I backpacked across Iceland, and explored Madagascar and Central Asia. In 2021 and 2023, I crossed Alaska on foot, packraft and bicycle, and we wrote a blog at north2arctic.com.
